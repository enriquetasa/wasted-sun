{% trans %}Why pay for the same electricity twice?{% endtrans %}

{% trans %} On sunny hours, the peninsula sometimes produces more solar power than the grid can absorb locally. That energy may go unused while other plants stay in the mix—so consumers can end up paying for flexibility and constraints twice over. {% endtrans %}

{% trans %} What helps? Stronger grids and storage over time—and market signals that reward using clean power when it is abundant, which can reduce waste and ease bills. {% endtrans %}
